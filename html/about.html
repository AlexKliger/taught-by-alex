Teaching Philosophy

A teacher doesn't just transfer knowledge to a student -- a quick internet search is capable of that. I believe that a teacher's most important role is to facilitate learning.

To that end, I utilize research-backed instructional strategies and classroom techniques that are proven to help students improve.

In practice however, there is no such thing as a one-size-fits-all method and I always adapt my approach to each student's unique learning style.

Qualifications

I graduated from the University of Miami where I received my B.S. in Biochemistry with a Minor in Mathematics. As an undergraduate student I discovered my interest in tutoring while helping other students in subjects that I did well in. I felt a sincere sense of fufillment when friends would reach that "Aha!" moment because a concept that they were tackling finally clicked.

What was once a part-time "gig" as a college student, grew into a full-time passion. I have been serving the South Florida community as a mathematics, science, and test prep tutor for over 5 years now. My experience has given me the opportunity to teach a broad range of subjects to students of various ages and backgrounds.

About Your Tutor

I am an active self-learner and always trying to expand my mental horizon. In my free time I enjoy coding, learning new languages, and taking hikes through nature with my dog Charlie.

I believe that education is a very empowering force in a person's life and I strive to help my students feel empowered.
